Sore throats, often caused by bacterial infections, can be effectively treated with antibiotics like penicillin or non-penicillin alternatives. Penicillin, a widely-used antibiotic, works by inhibiting bacterial cell wall synthesis, effectively eliminating the infection. Non-penicillin antibiotics, such as erythromycin or azithromycin, offer alternative treatment options, particularly for those allergic to penicillin. These antibiotics work by disrupting bacterial protein synthesis, halting the spread of infection. Consultation with a healthcare professional is crucial for proper diagnosis and antibiotic selection.
